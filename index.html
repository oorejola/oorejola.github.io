I am a researcher with an affinity for physics, computer science and mathematics. As an undergraduate at Univeristy of Colorado, I studied quantum computers and category theory. My Ph.D. thesis at Tulane focused on the asymptotic properties of high-dimensional fractional Brownian motion through the lens of wavelet eigen-analysis, as well as the consistency of machine learning techniques applied to these domains. I enjoy building things and solving problems. My overarching interest revolves around tackling problems within the domain of high-dimensional probability thoery and machine learning.
